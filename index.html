WEB3 & DIGITAL ASSETS

Curiosity is fine. Confusion is expensive.

Web3 yield products, staking, and "passive income" crypto offers get marketed with the same urgency as a scratch ticket. Here's the honest framing we teach in the guides.

What it actually is

Staking & on-chain yield

Returns from staking or liquidity provision come from network rewards, trading fees, or — in many "high APY" cases — new depositor funds. That last kind is not sustainable, no matter how the dashboard looks.

Before you fund anything

Custody questions to ask first

Who actually holds the keys — you, or the platform? Is the contract audited, by whom, and how recently? Can you exit instantly, or is there a lockup? If you can't answer these, you don't understand the product yet.

Red flag, not a feature

"Guaranteed" anything

No legitimate on-chain yield product can guarantee a fixed return — markets move, contracts get exploited, liquidity dries up. "Guaranteed 20% monthly" is the single most reliable scam indicator in this space.

What we actually recommend

Position sizing over prediction

The honest approach isn't picking the right coin — it's never putting in more than you can lose without it changing your life, and treating every projection as a guess with a number attached.
